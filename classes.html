Introduction to VR Development

Our introduction classes are designed to provide an overview of developing virtual reality applications in Unity. Each series is six weeks long, with one 2.5 hr lab per week covering topics around virtual and augmented reality conceptualization, design, and programming in Unity. The course walks participants through a number of important considerations for building VR experiences, resulting in a final application that participants will build for mobile VR over the course of the classes.

We are currently accepting applications for our Fall series in San Francisco (Nov-Dec) and Los Angeles (Oct-Nov). Apply Now!

VR Startup Bootcamp

The goal of the Startup Bootcamp is to give people knowledge and support around starting their own VR or AR startup. We will have a variety of well known industry speakers talking about all of the things you need to know when considering starting your own company. The series comprises four classes and a graduation event, where you will pitch your idea to actual investors and potential team members.

The cost of the VR Startup Bootcamp will be $100 to cover the expenses of running the event. You will be expected to be evaluating the material as you go through the class, and modify your ideas/pitches based on feedback. The goal of this class is to allow you to come away with the strongest possible pitch and pitch deck. We want your ideas to succeed, which means there will be work to do! If you apply with a cofounder and are accepted, we should be able to accommodate both of you. If your founding team is larger than 2, please decide which two team members would be most likely to benefit from these classes. If you don't have an idea yet, you can still apply, but please be aware that priority will go to those class members who will be most able to act upon the information they get in this extremely valuable class. However, people who are extremely passionate and knowledgeable about VR or AR will still be considered without an idea.

You will apply for the class via a One Minute Pitch Video. Talk about your idea, talk about why you’re the right person to create this idea. We don’t care about the production values of your video, and we don’t need to see fancy graphics. What we want to see is you, talking about your idea. 15-20 applicants will be chosen based on pre-decided criteria including enthusiasm, strength of idea, etc. It is extremely important to us that participants value the content and be able to use and apply everything they learn from our great mentors and instructors! Classes will be held in San Francisco on a weekly basis starting October 20th, from 7-9pm, with a break week for VRDC. Topics include building a business, raising money, scaling, sales, and growth, and pitching.

Applications are now open for the VR Startup Bootcamp, and will remain open through August 31st. As part of the form, we’ll ask you to link to a private password protected youtube video, which is no shorter than one minute, and no longer than 90 seconds, where you pitch your startup idea. We want to see what you have to say about your idea. Tell us about it, tell us about you, and focus on information rather than slick presentation - a webcam video of you talking passionately about your idea is going to be much more compelling to us than a slideshow where we never see your face. Consider answering the following questions: Why are you passionate about VR and AR? What do you hope to accomplish during your time at the VR Startup Bootcamp? What is your spirit device? - we want to know about you and what you want to build.

You will hear back about your application in mid-September. We're looking forward to helping grow great ideas!
